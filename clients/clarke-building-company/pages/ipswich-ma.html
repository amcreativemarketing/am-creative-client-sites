Renovating Ipswich's Historic Homes

First Period homes, built before 1720, are some of the most significant residential structures in America. Renovating them takes more than a general contractor's license. It takes experience working with original construction methods and respect for what makes these homes worth preserving.

Original Framing & Structure

Post-and-beam construction, hand-hewn timbers, and non-standard dimensions mean every cut and connection is custom. We assess structural integrity before recommending changes, so your renovation respects the bones of the home.

Local Historic Guidelines

Ipswich has specific requirements for homes in designated historic areas. We work within those guidelines, coordinating with local historic commissions so your project stays compliant from start to finish.

Hidden Infrastructure

Older homes often have surprises behind the walls: knob-and-tube wiring, uninsulated cavities, outdated plumbing runs. We plan for the unexpected and scope work carefully so there are fewer surprises once construction begins.

Modern Comfort, Historic Character

A kitchen renovation in a historic home requires custom solutions, not off-the-shelf approaches. We design interior upgrades that bring modern function without compromising the qualities that make these homes special.
