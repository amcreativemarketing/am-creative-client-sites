Structural Work and Room Reconfigurations

Older homes were built at a time when smaller, compartmentalized rooms were the standard. Today's homeowners typically want more open, connected living spaces. Getting there often requires structural work, and that's where having the right contractor matters.

  • Load-bearing wall removal: Opening up floor plans safely and correctly
  • Structural beam installation: Engineered beams and LVL work where walls come out
  • Foundation and framing repairs: Addressing structural issues common in older homes before finishing work begins
  • Room reconfigurations: Combining rooms, relocating doorways, reconfiguring staircase landings
  • Subfloor work: Leveling and repairing subfloors in homes that have shifted over time

We coordinate with structural engineers when required and pull all necessary permits. If you've been told a wall can't come out, or you're not sure, we're happy to take a look and give you an honest assessment.

Structural remodeling pairs naturally with larger-scope projects like home additions or ADU conversions when you're already working with walls and framing.

What a Clarke Building Renovation Covers

Every home renovation is different, but the level of detail stays the same. Here's the kind of work we handle as part of our remodeling projects:

  • Hardwood flooring: Installation of solid and engineered hardwood, refinishing and restoration of original floors, subfloor leveling and repair in older homes
  • Trim and millwork: Baseboards, crown molding, casing, chair rail, wainscoting, and coffered ceilings matched to your home's existing architectural details
  • Custom built-ins: Shelving, bookcases, window seats, mudroom storage, and entertainment centers built to fit the specific dimensions and style of your space
  • Interior doors: Pre-hung doors, pocket doors, and barn doors with proper framing and trim
  • Stair railings and balusters: Repair, replacement, and new installation
  • Built-in cabinetry: Custom storage solutions in living areas, home offices, and utility spaces
  • Window and door replacements: Integrated into surrounding finish work so new components blend with the existing home

For older homes with original architectural details worth preserving, we take care to match or complement what's already there. For homes that have lost their original character over the years, we help restore it.
