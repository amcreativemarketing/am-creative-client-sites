What a Kitchen Remodel with Clarke Building Actually Includes

Every kitchen project is different. Some homeowners need a complete tear-out and rebuild. Others are happy with the bones and just need updated surfaces. Clarke Building handles the full range, and here's what each type of work involves.

Full Gut Renovation

Everything comes out - cabinets, countertops, flooring, drywall, electrical, plumbing - and goes back in to a new plan. This is the right path when the kitchen is beyond cosmetic fixes, when you're changing the footprint, or when systems need to be brought up to code. Clarke Building coordinates all trades and manages the sequence start to finish.

Countertop Replacement

One of the highest-impact kitchen upgrades. Clarke Building installs quartz, granite, quartzite, butcher block, and solid surface countertops - including removal of the existing top, substrate repairs, templating, and final installation. Often paired with a backsplash update and new sink or faucet.

Layout Changes and Structural Work

A kitchen that doesn't flow right isn't fixed by new cabinets. Clarke Building handles wall removals, beam installations, island additions, and other structural changes that open up the space. Dave scopes this work accurately from the start so there are no mid-project surprises.

Appliance Upgrades and Coordination

New appliances often drive a remodel - a range that needs a gas line, a hood that requires custom framing, a fridge that changes the cabinet layout. Clarke Building plans rough-in locations, electrical circuits, and cabinet sizing together before demo starts so everything fits when it arrives.

What to Expect: Kitchen Renovation Budget Levels

Kitchen budgets vary widely based on scope. Here's a general framework to help you think about where your project might fall.

Surface Updates

New Cabinets and Countertops

Budget-Friendly

Replace cabinetry and countertops while keeping the existing layout and footprint. Typically includes new backsplash, hardware, and possibly a new sink or faucet. No walls move, no structural changes.

Layout Changes

Reconfigured Kitchen with Structural Work

Mid-Range

New cabinets and countertops plus layout adjustments. May include removing or opening a wall, adding or relocating an island, and updating electrical and plumbing to support the new configuration.

Full Renovation

Complete Gut and Rebuild

Premium Investment

Everything comes out and goes back in new. Structural changes, premium materials, full appliance package, new flooring, and updated mechanical systems. A project that transforms how the kitchen functions and connects to the rest of the home.

Note: These are general budget ranges to help you plan, not project quotes. Every kitchen is different, and factors like material selections, structural complexity, and existing conditions all affect final cost. The only way to get an accurate number for your kitchen is to have Dave walk the space.

How a Kitchen Remodel Works with Clarke Building

Kitchen remodeling has a lot of moving parts: trades, materials, lead times, permits. Here's exactly how Clarke Building runs a project from the first call to the last punch list item.

1

Initial Walkthrough

Dave walks your kitchen in person before any numbers get discussed. He evaluates the layout, cabinet condition, mechanical systems, and how the space connects to the rest of your home - and listens to what's not working for you.

2

Scope and Estimate

You'll receive a written scope and estimate organized by phase - not a ballpark. Where there are options (refacing vs. new cabinets, for example), each is priced separately so you can decide with real numbers.

3

Material Selection

Cabinetry, countertop material, backsplash, hardware, fixtures, and flooring all get locked in before demo starts. Lead times on custom cabinetry and specialty materials are factored into the schedule up front.

4

Demo and Rough-In

The existing kitchen comes out. Licensed plumbers and electricians handle rough-in work - new drains, supply lines, circuits, gas lines - along with any structural changes. Inspections are completed before the next phase begins.

5

Cabinet and Countertop Installation

Cabinets go in first, set level and secured to framing. Countertops are templated in place, fabricated, and installed with backsplash tile, sink setting, and trim work to follow. Clarke handles installation directly.

6

Appliances and Finish Work

Appliances are installed and connected. Finish electrical, finish plumbing, paint, and trim are completed by the licensed trades. Clarke stays on-site to coordinate the sequencing through completion.

7

Final Walkthrough

Dave walks the finished kitchen with you and a punch list. Every item - a door that doesn't close right, a gap that needs caulk, a drawer that needs adjustment - gets corrected before the final invoice is issued.
