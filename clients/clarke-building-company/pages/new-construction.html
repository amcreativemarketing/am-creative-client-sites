How New Construction Works with Clarke Building

Building a new home involves many moving parts: design, permits, foundation, framing, trades, inspections, and finishes. Here's exactly how I manage a new construction project from site selection through the final walkthrough.

1

Site Selection & Evaluation

Before any work begins, we walk the land together. I'm looking at slope, drainage, sun exposure, access, utilities, and how the house will sit on the property. We talk about your vision for the home and what the site itself suggests. If you're building in a development, we review lot restrictions and design guidelines. This conversation sets the direction for everything that comes next.

2

Design Coordination & Planning

I work with your architect or designer (or help you find one) to develop a plan that fits the site and your budget. We go over all options—house orientation, square footage, layout, finish levels—and talk through how each decision affects cost and timeline. When the design is locked, we pull permits and schedule inspections for each phase of construction.

3

Site Prep & Foundation

Once permits are in hand, we prepare the site: clearing, grading, and establishing access and utility staging areas. The foundation gets laid next. This is critical work—surveying, proper drainage, frost-protected footings, and careful concrete work. I coordinate inspections before moving forward. Any surprises in soil conditions or unexpected conditions get addressed now, not during framing.

4

Framing & Structural Systems

Framing is where the house takes shape. I manage the framing crew and coordinate mechanical rough-ins (electrical, plumbing, HVAC) as walls go up. This phase includes roof framing, sheathing, and initial weatherproofing. We schedule rough inspections for electrical and plumbing, and structural inspections if required. Coordination here prevents costly rework later.

5

Exterior Envelope & Weather Seal

Once framing is inspected, we install windows, roofing, and exterior cladding. The goal is to seal the house from weather so interior work can proceed. Flashing, siding, doors—all of this gets done carefully to prevent water intrusion. This is an important checkpoint. We walk the exterior together to confirm everything is done to standard before we close walls.

6

Interior Finishes & Mechanical Completion

With the envelope sealed, we move to interior work. Drywall goes up, tape and mud, painting. Finish electrical (outlets, fixtures, switches) and finish plumbing (faucets, fixtures, drains) are completed by licensed trades. Flooring installation, trim carpentry, and cabinet work happen during this phase. I coordinate the sequencing so nothing blocks the next step.

7

Final Inspections & Walkthrough

Before the certificate of occupancy is issued, the house passes final inspections. After that, we do a complete walkthrough together with a punch list in hand. Every detail—a sticky door, a gap that needs caulk, outlets that need covers—gets noted and corrected before the project closes. This is the standard that determines when the home is truly done.
