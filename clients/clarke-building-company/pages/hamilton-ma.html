A Rural Community with Room to Build

Hamilton is a town defined by space. Settled in 1638 and incorporated in 1793, it remains one of the most rural communities on the North Shore, with no industrial zoning, pastoral landscapes, stone walls along winding roads, and a strong equestrian heritage rooted in the Myopia Hunt Club and local horse farms.

For homeowners here, that character matters. Renovations and additions need to respect the scale of larger properties, match the architectural traditions of the area, and stand up to the New England climate. Clarke Building Company understands this balance. We are based in West Newbury, just a few miles away, and we bring the same care and attention to Hamilton properties that we bring to our own community.

Whether you are remodeling a kitchen in a Colonial farmhouse, adding living space to a family estate, or building a new home on a larger lot, we approach every Hamilton project with honest communication, transparent pricing, and craftsmanship you can see in the finished work.
