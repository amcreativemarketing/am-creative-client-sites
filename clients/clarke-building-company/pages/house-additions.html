Frequently Asked Questions About Home Additions in Massachusetts

How long does a home addition take?
Most home additions with Clarke Building Company take 3-6 months from permit approval to final walkthrough, depending on size and complexity. A structural bump-out may complete in 6-8 weeks; a full master suite or sunroom addition typically runs 3-5 months. We provide a detailed project timeline before work begins.
Do I need a permit for a home addition in Massachusetts?
Yes. Virtually all home additions in Massachusetts - including bump-outs, sunrooms, and room additions - require a building permit from your local town. Clarke Building Company handles the full permit application and inspection process for every project we build.
What is the difference between a sunroom and a 3-season room?
A sunroom is fully insulated and conditioned for four-season use, functioning as an extension of your heated and cooled living space. A 3-season room is designed for spring, summer, and fall use and typically uses screened or single-pane glazing without HVAC conditioning. Both add significant livability and value to your home.
How much does a home addition cost in Massachusetts?
Home addition costs in Massachusetts vary based on size, foundation requirements, complexity, and finish level. Master suite additions, sunrooms, and structural bump-outs each carry different cost profiles. We provide detailed, itemized estimates during our consultation process so you have full transparency on your investment.
Will the addition match my existing home?
Matching existing architecture is a core priority for Clarke Building Company. We carefully select siding, trim, windows, roofing, and interior finishes to blend with your existing home's character - whether you're adding onto a colonial, cape, craftsman, or contemporary home on the North Shore.
