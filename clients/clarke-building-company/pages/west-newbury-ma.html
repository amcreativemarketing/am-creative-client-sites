Working on Homes With Character and History

West Newbury's housing stock reflects the town's history. Many homes here were built before 1980, including colonials, capes, and saltboxes that carry real character but can also present challenges that newer construction doesn't have.

Homes on the North Shore frequently involve older framing techniques, aging wiring, older plumbing configurations, and foundation conditions that need assessment before structural work begins. We've worked on enough of these homes to know what to look for and how to approach the work responsibly.

Whether you have a cape that needs a full gut renovation or a colonial where you want to open up the floor plan, we take the time to understand what's behind the walls before we start closing them back up. When specialists like structural engineers or electricians need to be involved, we coordinate that as part of the project. Explore our renovation services to learn more.

Local Building Codes and Permitting in West Newbury

West Newbury has its own building department, and the town requires permits for most significant residential work including additions, decks, ADUs, structural renovations, and new construction. Skipping permits creates title problems, can void homeowner's insurance coverage, and in some cases requires removal and reconstruction at the owner's expense.

We handle all permitting on behalf of our clients. We know what West Newbury's building department requires, how to prepare submittals that move through review efficiently, and how to schedule inspections so projects don't stall. The permitting timeline is factored into every project schedule from day one.
