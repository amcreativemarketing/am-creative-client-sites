Common Electrical Problems in Plaistow, NH Homes

Plaistow's housing stock spans several decades - from mid-century ranch homes and Cape Cods built in the 1950s and '60s to colonial subdivisions from the 1980s and '90s and newer construction near Route 125. Each era comes with its own electrical issues, and Moore Electric has seen and repaired all of them.

Knob-and-Tube & Aluminum Wiring

Many older Cape Cods and ranches still contain original knob-and-tube or aluminum branch circuit wiring. Both present real safety risks - knob-and-tube is ungrounded and incompatible with modern load demands, and aluminum connections loosen over time creating fire hazards. Moore Electric assesses, repairs, and when necessary, replaces these systems to current code.

Overloaded Circuits & Tripping Breakers

Modern homes run far more load than their original design anticipated - EV chargers, home offices, mini-splits, and modern appliances push circuits sized for a fraction of that demand. Moore Electric diagnoses whether the issue is a true overload, a failing breaker, a ground fault, or a wiring problem - and provides the right fix, not a reset.

Flickering Lights

Persistent flickering - especially across multiple rooms or paired with buzzing sounds - can signal loose connections, a failing main breaker, or a utility service issue. In older neighborhoods, loose neutral connections at the panel or at aging junction boxes are a frequent culprit. Moore Electric traces flickering to its source rather than guessing.

Dead Outlets & Warm Receptacles

A dead outlet is often a tripped GFCI somewhere upstream - a quick fix. But warm outlets, discolored faceplates, or any burning smell near an outlet or panel are signs of active overheating and should be treated as urgent. Moore Electric treats any burning smell call as a priority repair.

Buzzing, Crackling & Sparking

Audible buzzing from a panel, crackling at outlets, or visible sparking when plugging in a device are all signs of loose connections, arcing wiring, or a failing breaker - conditions that can lead to electrical fires. Moore Electric responds to these calls promptly and performs a full diagnostic before closing out the job.

Outdated Panels

Homes with Federal Pacific, Zinsco, or undersized panels often experience persistent electrical issues that can't be fixed with individual repairs. When the panel itself is the root cause, Moore Electric recommends a panel upgrade to address the problem at its source.

Storm Damage Electrical Repairs in Plaistow, NH

Southern New Hampshire's storm season - from nor'easters and ice storms in winter to summer thunderstorms and the occasional tropical remnant - regularly causes electrical damage to homes. Moore Electric handles the full range of storm-related electrical repairs.

  • Surge DamageLightning strikes and utility switching events send voltage spikes through service entrances that blow breakers, kill outlets, and damage connected appliances. Brenden tests the full circuit path, not just the visible damage.
  • Service Entrance DamageHigh winds and falling limbs can damage the weatherhead, meter base, or service lateral. Moore Electric coordinates directly with Eversource or Unitil for utility disconnect and reconnect as part of the repair.
  • Generator-Related Wiring IssuesAfter extended outages, portable generator backfeed and improper transfer switch usage create wiring problems Moore Electric frequently diagnoses and corrects. Ask about standby generator installation for a permanent solution.
  • Flooded Basement CircuitsIce dam leaks and storm flooding can compromise basement wiring, sub-panels, and GFCI protection. Moore Electric assesses and restores these circuits safely before power is restored.

After any significant storm, if your home has tripped breakers that won't reset or visible damage to your service entrance, call Moore Electric for a post-storm assessment. As a licensed Master Electrician, Brenden provides documentation for insurance claims.

Common Repair Scenarios in Southern NH Homes

These are the types of electrical repair calls Moore Electric handles regularly throughout the area. Every diagnosis starts with understanding the root cause, not just treating the symptom.

Intermittent Outages in Half the House

Often a loose main neutral connection at the panel - a common failure in older homes. After ruling out the utility side, Moore Electric traces the problem to the service entrance neutral termination, tightens and seals the connection, and restores stable power throughout. Many homeowners live with this issue for months, resetting breakers without resolution.

Surge Damage After a Thunderstorm

A close lightning strike can kill several outlets, trip breakers that won't reset, and damage connected appliances. Moore Electric assesses the full panel, replaces failed AFCI breakers, restores dead circuits, and documents the electrical damage for the homeowner's insurance claim.

Aluminum Wiring Outlets Running Hot

Warm outlet covers and a burning smell traced to aluminum wiring connections that have loosened and oxidized at the device terminals - a well-documented failure mode for aluminum branch circuits. Moore Electric installs AlumiConn connectors at all affected device connections, eliminating the hazard without a full rewire.

Repair costs depend on the issue. A simple outlet replacement may run a few hundred dollars, while tracing and fixing a complex wiring fault or replacing a failed breaker panel costs more. A circuit breaker trips when it detects more current than the circuit is rated for, a short circuit, or a ground fault. The most common cause is circuit overload - too many appliances on one circuit. Repeated tripping can also signal a failing breaker or damaged wiring. If resetting doesn't hold, it's time for a professional diagnosis. In some cases, a panel upgrade is the right long-term fix.

Occasional flickering when a large appliance starts up is usually harmless. Persistent flickering - especially in multiple rooms or accompanied by buzzing - can indicate loose connections, a failing breaker, or a utility issue. These conditions can worsen over time and create fire risk. It's worth having a licensed electrician check it.

Stop using the outlet or switch immediately and turn off the breaker to that circuit. A burning or plastic smell near electrical components means something is overheating - this is a fire risk that requires urgent professional attention.
