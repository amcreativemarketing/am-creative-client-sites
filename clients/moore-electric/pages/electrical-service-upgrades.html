Understanding Service Upgrades

What Is an Electrical Service Upgrade?

An electrical service upgrade is not the same thing as a panel upgrade, though homeowners often confuse the two. Your electrical service includes everything from the utility pole or transformer to the meter base and service entrance cables that bring power into your home. A panel upgrade, on the other hand, replaces the breaker box inside.

Many homes - especially those built before the 1990s - need both. If your home still runs on 100-amp service, adding an EV charger, a modern HVAC system, a home addition, or a whole-home generator will likely require a full service upgrade to 200 amps. In some cases, the utility company will flag an aging overhead service entrance during a routine inspection or meter change, triggering the need for an upgrade.

Underground Service

Underground Electrical Service Change

An underground service change transitions your home's utility connection from an overhead service drop to a buried underground lateral. This is a common project for homeowners in Plaistow, NH and southern New Hampshire who want to improve storm resilience or update aging overhead wiring. It involves trenching a path from the utility connection point to your home, installing conduit, running new service conductors underground, and in some cases relocating the meter base.

Underground service changes require close coordination with your local utility - Eversource or Unitil - for disconnect, reconnection, and approval. Moore Electric manages that entire relationship on your behalf, from the initial application through final re-energizing.

When Do You Need an Underground Service Change?

  • Your overhead service drop is damaged, aging, or has been flagged by the utility
  • The utility company requires relocation during a renovation or property change
  • You want better storm resilience and a cleaner aesthetic without overhead wires
  • New construction with underground service planned from the start
  • Adding a detached garage or outbuilding that needs its own service run

What Does a Weatherhead Replacement Involve?

The weatherhead is the curved conduit at the top of your service entrance where the utility's service drop connects to your home. When upgrading or converting service, the weatherhead is typically replaced. Signs yours needs attention: visible rust, physical damage, or code non-compliance flagged during inspection.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions About Electrical Service Upgrades

What is an electrical service upgrade?

An electrical service upgrade replaces the components that deliver power from the utility to your home - the service entrance cables, meter base, weatherhead (for overhead service), and the service lateral (for underground). It increases your home's electrical capacity, typically from 100 amps to 200 amps. This is different from a panel upgrade, which only replaces the breaker box inside your house. Many older homes need both to safely support modern electrical loads.

How much does a 200-amp service upgrade cost?

The cost of a 200-amp service upgrade varies based on whether you have overhead or underground service, the length of cable runs, permit fees in your town, and any utility-specific requirements. Every home is different, which is why Moore Electric provides free on-site estimates with a detailed written quote before any work begins. Contact us to schedule a free estimate.

What's the difference between a service upgrade and a panel upgrade?

A service upgrade covers everything outside the house - the cables from the utility, the meter base, the weatherhead or underground lateral. A panel upgrade replaces the breaker box inside your home. Think of the service upgrade as upgrading the highway that brings power to your home, while the panel upgrade is upgrading the distribution center inside. Many homes need both, and Moore Electric can assess and handle both in the same project.

Do I need a permit for an electrical service upgrade in NH?

Yes. Every electrical service upgrade in New Hampshire requires an electrical permit from the local building department and a final inspection before the utility will reconnect power. Moore Electric handles the complete permit process - filing the application, scheduling the inspection, and making sure everything passes on the first visit.

How long does an electrical service upgrade take?

Most service upgrades are completed in one to two days of on-site work. The overall timeline is usually one to three weeks when you account for permit processing and utility scheduling. Underground service changes or overhead-to-underground conversions can take slightly longer due to trenching and additional utility coordination. Moore Electric keeps you informed at every step.

What is an underground service change?

An underground service change transitions your home's electrical supply from an overhead service drop (wires running from the utility pole to your house) to buried underground cables. This involves trenching, installing conduit, running new service conductors, and often relocating or replacing the meter base. Underground service is more resilient to storms and eliminates the visible wires on your home's exterior.

Should I choose underground or overhead electrical service?

Underground service offers better storm resilience, lower long-term maintenance, and a cleaner appearance with no visible wires. Overhead service has a lower upfront cost and is more widely available. The right choice depends on your property, budget, and what the local utility supports. If storm damage or aesthetics are a priority, underground is usually worth the investment. Brenden can walk you through both options during a free assessment.

What are signs I need an electrical service upgrade?

Common signs include: your home still has 60-amp or 100-amp service and you're adding major loads (EV charger, heat pump, home addition), visible rust or damage on your weatherhead or meter base, the utility has flagged your service entrance during a meter change, breakers trip frequently even after a panel evaluation, or you're planning generator installation that requires higher capacity. If you're experiencing any of these, a free assessment from Moore Electric can confirm whether an upgrade is needed.
