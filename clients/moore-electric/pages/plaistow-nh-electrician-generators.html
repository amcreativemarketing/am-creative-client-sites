Local Storm & Outage Context

Plaistow's Storm Season - Why Power Outages Here Aren't a "Maybe"

Plaistow and the surrounding southern NH communities sit in a particularly vulnerable position during New England's two major outage seasons. Here's what Moore Electric sees driving generator demand in Plaistow year after year.

Winter: Nor'easters & Ice Storms

The Merrimack Valley corridor runs directly through Plaistow and is one of the most reliably impacted zones during major nor'easters and ice events. Ice accumulation snaps limbs and poles, and Eversource NH restoration timelines in southern NH regularly stretch 3 to 7 days following significant events. The ice storms of 2008, 2017, and 2023 left parts of Rockingham County without power for nearly a week.

Spring: Wet Snow & Wind Events

Late-season nor'easters with heavy wet snow hit Plaistow's tree canopy hard, particularly in the wooded neighborhoods north of Route 125 and along the Hampstead town line. Wet snow on leafed-out trees in late April and May causes some of the region's most widespread damage to distribution lines.

Summer: Thunderstorm & Tropical Season

Severe convective storms and remnants of tropical systems have produced Plaistow outages ranging from hours to multiple days. Summer outages are particularly dangerous for homes with food storage, sump pumps, and basement flooding risk - all common in Plaistow's mix of older and newer residential construction.

Power outages in Plaistow are not an occasional inconvenience - they're a predictable seasonal event. A standby generator converts that exposure from a risk to a solved problem.

What a Whole-Home Generator Installation Includes in Plaistow

A standby generator installation in Plaistow is a multi-trade project. Moore Electric manages the electrical scope - transfer switch, load assessment, wiring - and coordinates directly with your gas or propane supplier and the Town of Plaistow Building Department. For general information about our generator services, visit our generator installation page.

1
Generator Sizing & Load Assessment Before any equipment is ordered, Moore Electric performs an on-site load assessment for your Plaistow home - calculating the circuits and appliances you want protected and sizing the generator appropriately. Undersized generators run at capacity under load and wear prematurely. Oversized units cost more than necessary. Getting the sizing right is the most important decision in the project.
2
Transfer Switch Installation The automatic transfer switch (ATS) is the brain of the system - it detects a utility outage within seconds and switches your home to generator power automatically, then transfers back when Eversource power is restored. Moore Electric installs the ATS adjacent to your main panel and wires it to the circuits identified in the load assessment. If your panel needs an upgrade to support the transfer switch, Moore Electric handles that as part of the project. Learn about panel upgrades in Plaistow.
3
Permit Filing with the Town of Plaistow Generator installations in Plaistow require both an electrical permit (filed with the Plaistow Building Department through Moore Electric's contractor license) and in most cases a gas permit coordinated with your gas supplier. Moore Electric files the electrical permit and coordinates the inspection timeline. A Plaistow building inspector performs the final sign-off.
4
Gas & Propane Coordination Standby generators in Plaistow run on either natural gas (available in some neighborhoods via Liberty Utilities) or propane (the more common option for Plaistow's rural and suburban properties). Moore Electric coordinates directly with your propane supplier for tank sizing and the gas line run to the generator pad. If you're on natural gas, Moore Electric works with your gas utility for the connection.

Investment & Savings

NH Generator Incentives, Rebates & Financing Options

A whole-home standby generator is a $5,000 to $15,000+ investment. Here's what Plaistow homeowners should know about reducing that cost.

NH & Eversource Incentive Programs

Eversource NH periodically offers demand response programs that include incentives for grid-connected backup generation assets. Some installations that incorporate smart transfer technology qualify for demand management enrollment. Moore Electric can advise on current program availability at the time of your project - these programs change annually.

Manufacturer Financing

Several generator manufacturers - including Generac and Kohler - offer financing programs for residential installations. Moore Electric can provide manufacturer financing information at the time of estimate. Many Plaistow homeowners also finance generator projects through home equity lines given the durable property value improvement a permanent standby unit provides.

Federal Tax Credits

Generators installed as part of a broader home energy system (paired with battery storage or solar) may qualify for federal residential clean energy credits. Standalone generator installations typically do not qualify for federal credits on their own.

Homeowners Insurance Discounts

Some NH insurance carriers offer premium discounts for homes with standby generators, particularly for properties in storm-exposed areas. Worth a call to your carrier after installation.
