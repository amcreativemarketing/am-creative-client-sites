Local Housing Context

Why Plaistow Homes Need Panel Upgrades

Plaistow's residential neighborhoods span several decades of construction - and each era brought its own electrical infrastructure that, in many cases, has reached the end of its serviceable life. Here's what Moore Electric sees most often.

1950s-70s

Mid-Century Homes Near Town Center

Older neighborhoods surrounding the town center and along routes like Westville Road and Plaistow Road contain homes still running on original fuse boxes or early breaker panels - including Federal Pacific Electric (FPE) Stab-Lok and Zinsco panels. Both have documented safety issues and are no longer acceptable under NH electrical code or most homeowners insurance carriers. Moore Electric regularly replaces FPE and Zinsco panels with modern, code-compliant 200-amp panels.

1980s-90s

Colonials & Split-Levels

Homes built in the Route 121A, Main Street, and Danville Road corridors were typically wired with 100-amp or 150-amp service - sized for the loads of that era. Today, those same homes are running EV chargers, central AC, heat pumps, and home offices simultaneously. Upgrading to 200-amp service is the standard solution, often combined with a service entrance upgrade to support the new panel.

2000s+

Newer Homes Near Route 125

Newer construction near Route 125 and in subdivisions off Kelley Road was built with 200-amp service - but panels still get upgrade calls. The most common: adding a Level 2 EV charger, wiring a home addition, or installing a whole-home generator that requires a transfer switch and dedicated circuit capacity.

Common Panel Upgrade Scenarios in Plaistow Homes

Utility Coordination

Working with Eversource for Your Plaistow Panel Upgrade

Eversource NH is the electric utility serving Plaistow. Any panel upgrade that requires a service disconnect - which includes all full panel replacements - must be coordinated with Eversource before work can begin. Moore Electric manages this entire process on your behalf.

  • Eversource Disconnect RequestFiled by Moore Electric on your behalf, typically 1-3 weeks lead time - longer during summer storm season. We schedule this the moment your permit is approved.
  • Day-Of CoordinationEversource disconnects at the meter in the morning. Moore Electric completes the panel replacement during the outage window. Eversource reconnects by end of day in most cases.
  • Meter Base AssessmentIf the existing meter base is outdated or doesn't meet Eversource's current specs, Moore Electric coordinates the replacement as part of the project to avoid a failed reconnect.
