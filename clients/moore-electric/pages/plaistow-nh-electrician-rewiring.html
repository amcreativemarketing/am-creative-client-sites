Insurance & Knob-and-Tube

Knob-and-Tube Wiring & NH Homeowners Insurance - What Plaistow Homeowners Need to Know

This is the issue that brings most Plaistow homeowners to Moore Electric's door. If you have knob-and-tube wiring, your insurance situation may already be urgent.

  • Policy refusals: NH insurance carriers are increasingly refusing to issue new policies on homes with active knob-and-tube wiring.
  • Non-renewal notices: Existing policyholders are receiving non-renewal letters with 30-60 day deadlines to address the wiring or lose coverage.
  • Home sale risk: Knob-and-tube is flagged as a material defect in NH home sales - it can stop a closing dead in its tracks.
  • Premium increases: Carriers that do still cover knob-and-tube charge significantly higher premiums for the added risk.
  • Moore Electric's documentation: We provide insurance-ready documentation with every rewiring project - permit numbers, scope of work, and final Plaistow Building Department inspection sign-off.

Permits & Inspections

NH Electrical Code & Plaistow Permit Requirements for Rewiring Projects

Every rewiring project in Plaistow requires a permit from the Plaistow Building Department. Moore Electric files all permits on your behalf - you don't need to visit Town Hall or manage the paperwork. Out-of-area contractors sometimes skip Plaistow permits. Moore Electric permits every job, no exceptions.

  1. 1
    Permit Application Moore Electric submits the electrical permit to the Plaistow Building Department, including the scope of work, contractor license number, and project details. This is filed before any work begins.
  2. 2
    Rough-In Inspection After new wiring is run but before walls are closed up, a Plaistow building inspector performs a rough-in inspection to verify all work meets the NH Electrical Code (NEC). This step is critical - it catches any issues while the wiring is still accessible.
  3. 3
    Final Inspection & Documentation After the project is complete, the Plaistow building inspector performs a final inspection and closes the permit. Moore Electric provides you with insurance-ready documentation - the permit number, scope of work, and signed-off inspection - so your carrier has exactly what they need to update your policy.

What Full and Partial Rewiring Projects Include in Plaistow

Not every older Plaistow home needs a full rewire. Moore Electric assesses your wiring condition, your goals, and your budget to recommend the right scope for your situation.

Full Whole-House Rewire

All existing wiring is replaced with modern copper wiring - new grounded outlets throughout, updated circuits, and full compatibility with a modern electrical panel. This is the standard recommendation for knob-and-tube and cloth-insulated homes.

  • All wiring replaced from panel to every device
  • New grounded outlets and circuits throughout
  • Modern panel compatibility
  • Complete insurance-ready documentation

Partial or Phased Rewiring

When a full rewire isn't required or the budget calls for a phased approach, Moore Electric can target the highest-priority circuits first - kitchen, bathroom, bedrooms - and address the rest over time.

  • Circuit-by-circuit prioritization
  • AlumiConn connector remediation for aluminum
  • Kitchen and bath priority upgrades
  • Pre-sale targeted replacement

Panel Upgrade Coordination

Many Plaistow rewiring projects also require a panel upgrade - especially homes with FPE (Federal Pacific) or Zinsco panels, which are known safety hazards. Moore Electric replaces the panel as part of the same project, avoiding the cost and disruption of two separate jobs. Learn about panel upgrades in Plaistow.
