Frequently Asked Questions About Vinyl Lettering

Common questions about materials, durability, and ordering.

Cast vinyl lasts five to seven years outdoors with proper installation. Calendered vinyl typically lasts three to five years. Both resist fading and water damage, but cast vinyl handles curves and extreme weather better over time.

Yes. Removable vinyl peels off cleanly from glass, painted walls, and most smooth surfaces without leaving residue or damage. Permanent vinyl can also be removed with heat and adhesive remover, though it takes more effort. We offer removable options for tenants and temporary applications.

Cut vinyl is solid-color vinyl that is cut into shapes by a plotter - the color goes all the way through the material. Printed vinyl is white vinyl with a full-color image printed on top and then laminated. Cut vinyl is more durable for text and single-color logos. Printed vinyl is used for full-color graphics and photos.
