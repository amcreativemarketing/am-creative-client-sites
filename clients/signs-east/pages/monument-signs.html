Freestanding Signs That Establish Your Presence

Monument signs are the ground-level, freestanding structures you see at building entrances, shopping centers, office parks, and residential communities. Unlike pole signs or building-mounted channel letters, monument signs sit close to the ground - typically under 8 feet tall - creating a solid, permanent presence.

They're often the first thing visitors see when arriving at your property. A well-designed monument sign tells people they're in the right place, reinforces your brand, and adds curb appeal that lasts for decades.

We design, fabricate, and install monument signs at our Kingston, NH shop. Whether you need a single-tenant sign for your business or a multi-tenant directory for a commercial plaza, we handle the entire project from permits to installation.

Types of Monument Signs We Build

Monument signs come in many styles. We'll help you choose based on your property, zoning requirements, and the look you want to achieve.

Masonry & Brick Monument Signs

Built with real brick, stone, or stone veneer to match your building's architecture. These signs convey permanence and quality - common for banks, medical facilities, professional offices, and upscale developments. We coordinate with masons or handle the full build in-house.

Aluminum Cabinet Signs

Fabricated aluminum structures with push-through or applied graphics. Lightweight, durable, and ideal for illuminated signs. These work well for retail centers, gas stations, and businesses that need bright nighttime visibility. Easy to update tenant panels on multi-tenant versions.

HDU & Carved Foam Signs

High-density urethane (HDU) can be carved, routed, and painted to look like wood, stone, or any custom shape. Lighter and more affordable than real masonry, but still built to last outdoors. Popular for HOA entrance signs, churches, and businesses wanting a classic carved look.

Multi-Tenant Directory Signs

Shopping centers, office parks, and commercial plazas need signs that list multiple businesses. We build these with interchangeable tenant panels so names can be swapped when leases change. Illuminated options ensure every tenant gets visibility day and night.

Why Monument Signs Work

There's a reason property owners invest in permanent ground signage.

Wayfinding

Visitors can find you from the street without slowing down or second-guessing. A clear monument sign at your entrance reduces confusion and missed turns.

Permanence

Ground-mounted signs communicate stability. Unlike banners or temporary signage, a monument sign says you're established and here to stay.

Curb Appeal

A quality monument sign adds value to your property. It's one of the first things people notice - and remember - about your location.

Illumination Options

Most monument signs benefit from lighting - either internal illumination (LED-lit cabinet faces or channel letters) or external ground-mounted spotlights. We use commercial-grade LEDs rated for 50,000+ hours. If you have an older sign with fluorescent or neon lighting, we can retrofit it to LED for lower energy costs and better reliability.

How a Monument Sign Project Works

From first call to finished installation - here's what to expect.

1

Site Survey & Consultation

We visit your property to assess the location, measure setbacks, check for underground utilities, and understand your goals. We'll discuss materials, size options, illumination, and any HOA or landlord requirements. You get a detailed quote with no hidden costs.

2

Design & Engineering

Our designers create scaled renderings showing how your sign will look on-site. For larger signs, we provide engineered drawings specifying foundation requirements and wind load calculations. You approve the design before we move to permitting.

3

Permitting & Fabrication

Monument signs almost always require permits - sign permits, sometimes building permits for the foundation, and electrical permits for illuminated signs. We handle all the paperwork. While permits are processed, we fabricate your sign in our shop.

4

Foundation & Installation

Once permits are approved, we pour the concrete foundation (or coordinate with your contractor), then install the sign structure, graphics, and electrical. We handle final inspections and walk you through everything before we leave.
