Portsmouth Business Districts We Serve

From the waterfront to the Tradeport, we know where Portsmouth businesses are and what kind of signage each area demands.

Downtown and Market Square

The walkable heart of Portsmouth - restaurants, retail, galleries, and tourism along Market Street, Congress Street, and the waterfront. Historic District Commission review is required for exterior sign changes in this area, adding roughly a month to the approval timeline. We've navigated the process before.

Pease International Tradeport

Former Air Force base turned commercial hub with 300+ companies and 9,000+ employees. Tech firms, manufacturing, logistics, and Portsmouth International Airport. Separate land use controls through the Pease Development Authority. Economic Revitalization Zone with tax credits.

Route 1 / Lafayette Road

Major north-south commercial corridor connecting Portsmouth to Rye, Hampton, and Seabrook. Retail plazas, car dealerships, restaurants, and service businesses. High-traffic locations ideal for monument signs, digital readerboards, and large-format building signage.

Heritage-Constitution Industrial Park

Industrial zone along Route 1 with manufacturing, warehousing, and distribution facilities. Also an ERZ zone and part of Foreign Trade Zone #81. Building-mounted signage, directional wayfinding, and fleet graphics for companies running out of this area.

Islington Street and West End

Mixed-use corridor connecting downtown to the West End redevelopment area. Small businesses, restaurants, creative studios. The West End received ERZ designation in 2018, attracting new commercial development and signage needs.

Newington and Kittery Corridor

The adjacent commercial areas across the border - Fox Run Mall, Kittery outlets, and the Route 1 Bypass corridor. We serve businesses on both sides of the Piscataqua River.

What Are the Sign Regulations in Portsmouth?

Portsmouth has specific sign regulations under Article 12 of the Zoning Ordinance. If your business is in the Historic District (most of downtown), exterior sign changes also require approval from the Historic District Commission, which meets monthly and adds about a month to the process.

We've installed signs across Southern NH and the Seacoast and can walk you through what's required for your specific location and sign type. One less thing to figure out on your own.
