Construction Safety and Warning Signs

Hard hat required, PPE zones, danger, caution, and warning signs produced to ANSI Z535 safety color standards. The correct color coding matters - red means danger (immediate hazard), orange means warning (potential hazard), yellow means caution (minor hazard). We get it right so your site passes inspection. Available in rigid aluminum, coroplast, and self-adhesive vinyl.

Frequently Asked Questions About Construction Signs

Common questions about materials, sizing, and ordering.

We use aluminum composite, heavy-gauge aluminum, and coroplast depending on the application. Aluminum is best for permanent or long-term installations. Coroplast works well for temporary construction signs that need to be lightweight and portable. All materials are printed with UV-resistant inks for outdoor durability.

Coroplast temporary signs typically hold up for 6 to 12 months of outdoor exposure depending on conditions. Aluminum signs last significantly longer - three to five years or more. Both options use UV-resistant inks that resist fading from sun exposure.

We produce standard sizes including 18x24, 24x36, 36x48, and 48x48 inches, plus any custom dimension your project requires. Road construction signs follow DOT standard sizing. Give us the specs and we will build to match.
