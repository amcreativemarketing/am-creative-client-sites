Exeter Commercial Districts We Serve

Whether your business is on Water Street, the Epping Road corridor, or out by the industrial park - we know Exeter and we know what it takes to get your signage noticed, permitted, and installed.

Downtown Exeter

Water Street, Front Street, Court Street, Center Street, and Bow Street. Exeter's historic heart with foot traffic, restaurants, and local retailers. Sign regulations in the WC and C-1 zones apply - we can help navigate those requirements.

Epping Road Corridor

A growing commercial strip with C-3 zoning and tax incentives through the Economic Revitalization program. High visibility for monument signs, pylon signs, and storefront lettering.

Portsmouth Avenue

Highway Commercial (C-2) zone along one of Exeter's busiest routes. Ideal for large-format signage, illuminated signs, and digital readerboards that capture traffic heading toward the Seacoast.

Lincoln Street

Near Exeter's Downeaster Amtrak stop - the busiest in NH. Local businesses here benefit from commuter and school traffic. Vinyl graphics, A-frame sidewalk signs, and window displays work well in this area.

Industrial and Professional Parks

Holland Way, Route 88, and Hampton Road. Larger lots for monument signs, building-mounted signage, and wayfinding systems for office complexes and manufacturing facilities.

Surrounding Areas

What Does the Sign Permit Process Look Like in Exeter?

Exeter requires a sign permit for most commercial signage installations. The rules vary by zoning district - downtown's historic overlay has stricter requirements than the Epping Road corridor, for example.

Most business owners don't want to deal with building applications, zoning codes, or scheduling inspections. That's where we come in. Signs East handles the permit coordination for your project so you can focus on running your business.

We've worked with towns across Southern NH and the Seacoast. We know the process, we know the people, and we'll make sure your sign is built to code and installed on schedule.
