Types of Dimensional Signs We Build

Each style has its place. We'll help you pick the right one for your location, budget, and visibility needs.

Illuminated Channel Letters

The classic three-dimensional business sign. Each letter is individually fabricated and lit from within using LED modules. Front-lit, back-lit (halo), or both - your sign works day and night. Built to last 10+ years with minimal maintenance.

Flat Cut Letters

Precision-cut from acrylic, aluminum, PVC, or brushed metals. Clean, modern, and versatile. Perfect for interior lobbies, office walls, and directional signage where you want professional depth without illumination.

LED Backlit Signs

Letters mounted slightly off the wall with LEDs behind them, creating a dramatic halo glow. High-end look that's popular for restaurants, hotels, and businesses wanting an upscale nighttime presence. We use commercial-grade LEDs rated for 50,000+ hours.

Dimensional Plaques & Cast Signs

Raised letters and graphics on a solid backing - bronze, aluminum, or HDU foam. Classic look for building dedications, historical markers, donor walls, and professional directories. Built to last generations.

How a Channel Letter Project Works

From first call to final installation - here's what to expect.

1

Consultation & Site Survey

We visit your location to measure, photograph, and understand your goals. We'll discuss materials, illumination options, mounting methods, and any landlord or HOA requirements. You get a detailed quote with no surprises.

2

Design & Approval

Our designers create scaled mockups showing exactly how your sign will look on your building. You'll see font options, color choices, and day/night visibility. We revise until you're happy - then lock in the design for production.

3

Permitting & Fabrication

We handle the permit applications, drawings, and approvals with your local building department. While paperwork moves, we start fabricating your sign in our shop. Permitting often takes longer than fabrication - every town is different.

4

Installation & Walkthrough

Our crew handles the installation - lift equipment, electrical connections, final alignment. We test everything on-site and walk you through operation and maintenance. You get documentation and our direct number if anything ever needs attention.
