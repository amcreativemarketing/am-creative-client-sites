Home Addition Questions? We've Got Answers.

Do I need permits for a home addition?
Yes. All additions require building permits, and most require review by the local planning or zoning board. In historic districts, additional approvals may apply. We coordinate the entire permit process, including applications, plans, and town meetings if required.
What if you discover hidden issues when you open the walls?
Older homes sometimes have surprises: outdated wiring, inadequate framing, or previous repairs done poorly. When we encounter issues, we discuss options and costs with you before proceeding. Our detailed discovery process minimizes surprises, but we plan for contingencies and communicate openly when they arise. No work proceeds without your approval.
What's the difference between design-build and hiring an architect separately?
With design-build, one company coordinates both design and construction. This creates accountability, streamlines communication, and typically reduces cost and timeline. When you hire an architect separately, you're managing two relationships and hoping they work well together. Design-build works especially well for additions, where construction realities need to inform design decisions from the start.
How do I know if my lot can accommodate an addition?
Setback requirements, lot coverage limits, and height restrictions vary by town and zone. We research these constraints early in the process. Sometimes a creative design solution works around tight restrictions. Sometimes the numbers just don't work. We'll give you an honest assessment during your discovery call before you invest in detailed plans.
What areas do you serve?
We serve the entire North Shore and Merrimack Valley, including: Amesbury, Newburyport, Newbury, West Newbury, Salisbury, Georgetown, Groveland, Rowley, Boxford, Merrimac, and surrounding communities. Based in Amesbury, we typically work within a 30-minute radius. Contact us if you're unsure whether we serve your area.
