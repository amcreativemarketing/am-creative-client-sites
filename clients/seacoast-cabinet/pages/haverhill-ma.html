How to Choose the Right Materials and Styles for Your Kitchen Cabinets

Selecting the right cabinet material and finish is one of the most important decisions in a kitchen remodel. The combination affects durability, maintenance, appearance, and cost. At our Rowley showroom, you can see and touch samples in person before making a decision.

What Are Popular Cabinet Materials Like Maple, Oak, and Cherry?

Maple is the most popular choice among our Haverhill clients. It has a smooth, consistent grain that takes paint and stain evenly, making it versatile for both modern and traditional kitchens. It's also one of the hardest domestic woods, so it resists dents and scratches well.

Oak offers a more prominent grain pattern and is a great fit for homeowners who want visible wood character. It's extremely durable and handles Massachusetts humidity well. Cherry is a premium option that deepens in color over time, developing a rich patina that many homeowners find appealing.

We also carry engineered and thermofoil options through our Mantra line for clients who want a quality cabinet at a lower price point. These materials have improved significantly in recent years and offer solid performance for the investment.

Which Kitchen Cabinet Finishes and Hardware Options Are Best?

Painted finishes in white, warm gray, and navy remain the top picks across the North Shore. Stained finishes in natural and espresso tones are making a comeback, especially in transitional-style kitchens. Many homeowners choose a two-tone approach, pairing painted perimeter cabinets with a stained island.

Hardware sets the tone for the whole kitchen. Brushed gold and matte black pulls are trending in 2026, while brushed nickel remains a reliable classic. Our team can help you pair hardware styles to your door profile for a cohesive look. Visit our showroom to see the options in person or explore our cabinet lines online.
