Shaker Cabinets

Clean, recessed center panel framed by a simple rail-and-stile border. No ornate carvings, no fussy detailing - just balanced proportions and honest craftsmanship that works in any home.

Why Homeowners Choose Shaker

  • Works in traditional, transitional, or modern kitchens
  • Low maintenance and easy to clean
  • Dozens of colors and wood species available
  • Pairs with granite and Cambria quartz countertops
  • The most requested door style in America
Best for: Any style home Price: Mid-range

Raised Panel Cabinets

A three-dimensional center panel that sits above the surrounding frame, creating depth and shadow detail. The result is a richer, more formal appearance built for traditional homes.

Why Homeowners Choose Raised Panel

  • Multiple door profiles from subtle to highly detailed
  • Cherry, Maple, and painted finishes available
  • Pairs with crown molding, corbels, and decorative accents
  • Full overlay and inset configurations through our cabinet lines
  • Ideal for colonials, Georgians, and traditional estates
Best for: Traditional homes Price: Mid to premium

Slab Cabinets

A single, flat panel with no frame lines, bevels, or ornamentation. Minimal, streamlined, and intentionally modern - delivering visual calm that busier door profiles can't match.

Why Homeowners Choose Slab

  • Painted MDF, wood veneer, thermofoil, or high-gloss lacquer
  • Frameless (European-style) construction available
  • Very easy to clean - flat surface, no crevices
  • Creates open, airy feel in any size kitchen
  • Growing demand across coastal and urban areas
Best for: Contemporary homes Price: Budget to premium

Choosing the Right Cabinet Style for Your Home

Shaker vs. Raised Panel - The Most Common Question

The choice between Shaker and Raised Panel usually comes down to the architectural style of your home and the level of detail you prefer. Raised Panel doors add visual weight and formality that suits colonials, Georgians, and traditional estates across Haverhill and Topsfield. Shaker offers a lighter, more versatile profile that works everywhere - from Cape Cod cottages in Ipswich to colonial farmhouses in Georgetown.

The Rise of Slab Cabinets in New England

There's growing demand for clean-line kitchen design across the North Shore and Southern New Hampshire. Homeowners in Newburyport, Amesbury, and Salem, NH are increasingly pairing slab doors with frameless construction for a seamless, furniture-like appearance.
