Common Questions About Our Design Services

Everything you need to know about working with our design team.

Do I need to come to the showroom or can you design remotely? +

We offer both options! Many clients prefer visiting our Rowley showroom to see and touch cabinet samples in person. But if that's not convenient, we can come to your home for measurements and initial consultation, then work with you virtually to refine the design.

How long does the design process take? +

Most clients receive their 3D renderings and project estimate within just a few days of the initial consultation. If you need revisions, those typically take 1-2 business days. The entire process from consultation to final specifications usually takes 1-2 weeks, but we can work faster if your project timeline requires it.

Will you work with my contractor? +

Absolutely! We provide contractor-ready CAD drawings and detailed specifications your builder needs for accurate installation. We can also coordinate directly with your contractor on timeline, delivery, and any technical questions that come up during the project.

What if I'm not sure about my budget yet? +

That's completely normal! During your consultation, we'll discuss budget ranges and show you examples at different price points. We work with all three cabinet brands (Mantra, Homecrest, and Omega) which gives us flexibility to design within virtually any budget.

Do you help with countertop and hardware selection too? +

Yes! While our primary focus is cabinetry, we provide expert guidance on countertops, hardware, and finishes that complement your cabinet choice. We want your entire kitchen to feel cohesive and work within your budget. Learn more about our countertop options.

Can you design bathrooms and other spaces too? +

Definitely! While kitchens are our specialty, we also design beautiful bathroom vanities, home office cabinetry, mudrooms, laundry rooms, and more. Any space that needs custom cabinetry, we can design.

What if I decide not to move forward after the design? +

The initial consultation is free, so there's no obligation. If you commission a full design ($295) but decide not to move forward with the project, the design fee remains - but it's a small investment to get professional renderings and accurate pricing. However, most clients who invest in design do move forward, and that fee gets credited to their purchase anyway.
