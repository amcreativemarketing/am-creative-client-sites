Types of Chain Link Fencing We Install

Olympic Fence Co. installs a full range of chain link fencing systems across Massachusetts. Each material option is suited to different applications, budgets, and aesthetic preferences. Our team will recommend the right choice for your property during your free on-site estimate.

Galvanized Chain Link Fencing

The most popular and affordable chain link option. Zinc-coated galvanized steel resists corrosion and performs well in Massachusetts' freeze-thaw climate. Best for perimeter fencing, commercial enclosures, sports facilities, and budget-conscious residential projects. Available in all standard heights and gauges.

Vinyl-Coated Chain Link Fencing

Black, green, or brown PVC coating gives vinyl-coated chain link a finished, polished look that blends with residential landscapes. Popular in Massachusetts neighborhoods where curb appeal matters. The vinyl layer adds extra corrosion protection beyond the galvanized steel core. Available in all heights and gauges.

Considering other fencing materials for your property? We also install vinyl fencing, cedar fencing, ornamental aluminum and steel fencing, and privacy fencing across Massachusetts.

Residential Chain Link Fence Installation

Olympic Fence Co. installs residential chain link fencing across Massachusetts for a wide range of applications. Chain link is one of the most versatile and cost-effective fencing options available - it defines property lines, keeps pets safe, protects gardens, and encloses pools at a fraction of the cost of wood or vinyl.

Standard residential chain link is available in 3-foot to 6-foot heights. Four-foot fencing works well for yard definition and garden protection. Pet, Dog, and Child Safety Fencing

Chain link is one of the most durable options for dog containment - it resists scratching and chewing that would damage wood fencing. We offer anti-dig bottom tension wire, self-closing spring-loaded gates, and custom dog runs sized to your yard. For pool areas, our chain link fencing meets Massachusetts pool safety codes with compliant heights and self-closing gate hardware.

Common Chain Link Fence Damage in New England

Massachusetts freeze-thaw cycles are hard on fence posts - heaving, cracking, and leaning are common after harsh winters. Storm damage from fallen tree branches and vehicle strikes can bend framework and tear mesh. Rust and corrosion on older galvanized fencing can spread if left untreated.

Repair vs. Replace

In many cases, targeted repairs are faster and more cost-effective than a full fence replacement. Our repair work includes post straightening and replacement, fabric re-tensioning, gate realignment, section replacement, and hardware upgrades. We'll assess the damage and give you an honest recommendation on whether repair or replacement makes the most sense for your situation.

Chain Link Fence Wire Gauge - What's Right for Your Project?

Wire gauge determines the thickness and strength of your chain link fence fabric. A lower gauge number means thicker, stronger wire. Olympic Fence stocks all standard gauges and will recommend the right option for your specific application and budget.

Gauge Wire Diameter Best For
6-gauge Thickest Industrial facilities, high-security perimeters, restricted access areas
9-gauge Heavy-duty Commercial properties, business perimeters, equipment yards
11-gauge Standard Residential fencing, pet containment, general property enclosures
11.5-gauge Light Temporary fencing, construction site enclosures

For most Massachusetts residential projects, 11-gauge galvanized chain link is the standard. Commercial and business properties typically benefit from 9-gauge for extra strength. Industrial and high-security applications call for 6-gauge heavy-duty wire.
