Amesbury Fence Regulations - What You Should Know

  • Amesbury has a Board of Fence Viewers that advises property owners before construction
  • Maximum fence height is 6 feet (stricter than many Massachusetts towns)
  • Permits required for fences within front, side, or rear setback areas
  • Boundary fences (within 5 feet of property line) need a separate permit
  • Fences near intersections: max 3 feet high, 5-foot setback for visibility

Do I need a fence permit in Amesbury, MA?

Amesbury has a maximum fence height of 6 feet, which is stricter than many surrounding towns. Fences near property boundaries or setback lines may require a permit and review by the Board of Fence Viewers. Fences at intersections must also maintain visibility for traffic safety. Olympic Fence is familiar with all of Amesbury's specific requirements and handles the permitting process for you.

What is Amesbury's Board of Fence Viewers?

The Board of Fence Viewers is a local regulatory body in Amesbury that reviews fence disputes and certain fence installations near property boundaries. They ensure fencing meets town standards for height, placement, and neighborhood compatibility. Olympic Fence has worked with the Board for years and understands how to navigate the review process smoothly.
