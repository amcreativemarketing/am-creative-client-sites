Ground Coring Services

Specialized Service

What It Is

Ground coring creates precise holes in frozen, rocky, or difficult ground conditions to install fence posts, bollards, and structural supports. This specialized technique uses industrial drilling equipment to bore through concrete, ledge, frozen soil, and other challenging substrates that traditional post hole digging cannot handle.

Our Process

  • Site assessment for soil conditions and utilities
  • Equipment selection (6"-24" diameter capabilities)
  • Precision drilling to specified depths (36"-48" typical)
  • Post installation with proper concrete fill
  • Site restoration and debris removal

Bollard Installation & Repair

Safety & Protection

What It Is

Bollards are sturdy vertical posts designed to protect buildings, equipment, pedestrians, and property from vehicle impact. Essential for storefronts, loading docks, utility equipment, and high-traffic areas. We install new bollards and repair damaged existing installations.

Our Process

  • Protection planning and placement recommendations
  • Foundation preparation (36"-42" deep)
  • Steel pipe bollards (4"-12" diameter), concrete-filled
  • Safety paint and reflective tape as needed
  • Repair services: straightening, re-setting, replacement

Concrete Anchoring & Fastening

Structural Installation

What It Is

Concrete anchoring secures posts, equipment mounts, safety rails, and structural elements to existing concrete slabs, floors, and foundations. This includes expansion anchors, epoxy anchoring systems, and through-bolt installations for warehouse guardrails, equipment bases, and retrofit work.

Anchor Types

Expansion Anchors Standard applications, quick install
Epoxy Anchoring High-load, cracked concrete
Wedge Anchors Heavy-duty, permanent
Through-Bolts Equipment, removable

Parking Lot Sign Installation

Compliance & Safety

What It Is

Parking lot signage includes regulatory signs (handicapped parking, fire lanes, no parking zones), directional signs, tenant parking assignments, and safety warnings. Professional installation ensures signs remain secure through New England weather while meeting ADA and local code requirements.

Our Process

  • Site survey and ADA compliance review
  • Foundation installation (minimum 30" deep)
  • Code-compliant mounting (60"-80" height)
  • Coordinate with striping contractors
  • Repairs: storm damage, faded panels, rust
