Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about residential fence installation in Massachusetts.

Residential fence costs vary based on material, height, length, and site conditions. Cedar fencing typically offers the best entry price point, vinyl provides excellent long-term value with zero maintenance, and our exclusive wood-on-steel system delivers premium durability. Massachusetts installation costs generally run 15-25% higher than national averages due to our soil conditions and weather requirements. We provide detailed, transparent quotes with options to fit different budgets.

In most North Shore towns, fences 6 feet or under don't require permits for backyard installation. Front yards are typically limited to 3-4 feet, and corner lots may have sight-line restrictions. Pool fences must meet specific safety requirements. Don't worry about the details—we handle all permit applications and know the specific requirements for each town we serve.

Most residential fence installations take 2-4 days, depending on length and site conditions. A typical 150-200 linear foot fence is usually completed in 3 days. We provide a clear timeline upfront and stick to it. Current scheduling is typically 3-4 weeks out during our busy season (late summer through fall).

Traditional wood posts set in concrete will eventually rot at ground level—it's the number one cause of fence failure. Our wood-on-steel system uses galvanized steel posts with cedar panels attached above ground, eliminating ground-level rot entirely. This gives you the natural beauty of cedar with dramatically improved longevity (30+ years vs. 15-20 for traditional). You won't find this system offered by other contractors on the North Shore.

Vinyl privacy fences last 35+ years with zero maintenance. Our wood-on-steel system lasts 30+ years by preventing ground-level rot. Traditional cedar lasts 15-30 years with periodic staining. Chain-link and aluminum fences can last 25+ years. We'll help you choose based on your priorities: aesthetics, maintenance preferences, and budget.

Yes, we specialize in challenging installations. Whether your property has slopes, ledges, rocky soil, or uneven terrain, we have solutions. We can step the fence, follow the contour, or combine techniques for the best appearance and function. Many competitors won't tackle difficult properties—we see them as opportunities to showcase our 40+ years of expertise.
