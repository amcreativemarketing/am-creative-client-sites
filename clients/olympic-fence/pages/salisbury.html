Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a permit for a fence in Salisbury, MA? ▼

In Salisbury, fences 6 feet or under typically don't require a building permit for residential properties. Fences over 6 feet generally need approval from the Building Department. Setback requirements may also apply depending on your property's location.

We handle all permit guidance as part of our service and will let you know exactly what's required during your estimate.

How much does fence installation cost in Salisbury? ▼

Fence costs in Massachusetts typically run 15-25% higher than national averages due to labor costs and material availability. Cedar/wood fences generally range from $21-$50 per linear foot installed, while vinyl runs $30-$60 per linear foot. Chain link is the most economical option.

Property terrain, fence height, gate requirements, and total length all impact final pricing. We provide detailed, itemized estimates with multiple options. Contact us for a free on-site estimate specific to your property.

What fence materials work best for coastal properties? ▼

For properties near Salisbury Beach or exposed to salt air, we recommend coastal-grade vinyl fencing or our proprietary wood-on-steel cedar system. Both handle moisture, freeze-thaw cycles, and salt exposure exceptionally well.

Vinyl offers 35+ years maintenance-free performance. Wood-on-steel combines the natural beauty of cedar boards with steel posts that resist rot, insects, and ground movement.

How long does fence installation take? ▼

Most residential fence installations take 2-5 days depending on length, material, and property conditions. A typical 200-foot privacy fence can often be completed in 3-4 days.

Current lead times from estimate to installation average 2-4 weeks during peak season (late summer through fall). Off-season installations often have faster scheduling. We provide a specific timeline during your consultation.

What does your lifetime workmanship warranty cover? ▼

Our lifetime workmanship warranty covers defects in installation for as long as you own your home. This includes post settling, gate misalignment due to installation errors, and structural failures related to how the fence was built.

Most competitors only offer 2-3 year warranties on their work. Our lifetime coverage reflects our confidence in our installation quality. Material warranties are provided separately by manufacturers.
