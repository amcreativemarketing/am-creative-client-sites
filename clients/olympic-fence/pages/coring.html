What is Coring?

Coring (also called core drilling) is a specialized technique that creates precise, cylindrical holes through hard surfaces like concrete, stone, asphalt, or retaining walls to install fence posts securely.

Unlike surface-mounted brackets that bolt onto concrete (and can loosen over time), coring embeds your fence posts directly INTO the concrete - creating a bond that lasts decades, not years.

Why Coring is Stronger Than Surface Mounting

Surface-mounted fence posts rely on 4 small bolts that can work loose from wind pressure and daily use. Cored posts are embedded in solid concrete and secured with high-strength hydraulic cement that actually bonds stronger than the original concrete. It's the same technique used in bridge construction.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our coring and fence installation services.

How long does coring take?
Most residential coring jobs take just a few hours. We can typically core, set posts, and complete your fence installation in 1-2 days depending on the project size.
Will coring crack my concrete patio?
No. Our diamond core drill creates clean, precise holes without cracking or damaging the surrounding concrete. We use water-cooled equipment that minimizes stress on the material.
Is coring more expensive than regular fence installation?
Coring involves specialized equipment and expertise, so there is an additional cost compared to standard soil installation. However, it's typically far less expensive than removing and replacing your patio to install fence posts the traditional way.
Can you core through asphalt driveways?
Yes. We can core through concrete, asphalt, brick, stone, and block surfaces.
Do I need a permit for fence installation on my patio?
In most Massachusetts towns, fences under 6 feet don't require a permit regardless of installation method. We'll help you verify local requirements during your consultation.
Can any fence type be installed using coring?
Yes. We can install vinyl, wood, chain link, aluminum, and ornamental iron fences using the coring method. We'll recommend the best material based on your needs and budget.
How does the hydraulic cement hold up in New England weather?
Excellent. Non-shrink hydraulic cement is designed for freeze-thaw cycles and actually bonds stronger than regular concrete. It's the same material used in bridge construction.
Can you install gates using the coring method?
Absolutely. In fact, gate posts benefit most from coring because they need extra stability to handle the daily stress of opening and closing.
