Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about dog fencing installation in Massachusetts.

Fence height depends on your dog's size, breed, and jumping ability. For small to medium dogs (under 40 lbs), a 4-5 foot fence is typically sufficient. For large or athletic breeds like German Shepherds, Labs, or Huskies, we recommend 6 feet minimum. Some breeds (Huskies, Malamutes, athletic terriers) may require 6-8 feet. During our free consultation, we assess your specific dog's behavior and recommend the appropriate height. Massachusetts regulations typically allow 6-foot fences in backyards without permits in most towns.

We use several proven dig-prevention techniques: (1) Installing fence posts deeper than standard (minimum 30 inches), (2) Adding a concrete footer or gravel base along the fence line, (3) Burying fence fabric 6-12 inches below ground level, (4) Installing L-shaped footer barriers for persistent diggers, and (5) Ensuring zero gaps between fence bottom and ground level, even on slopes. For breeds known for digging (terriers, hounds, northern breeds), we recommend our reinforced dig barrier system that extends 18 inches below ground.

Each material has advantages depending on your priorities: Cedar privacy fencing provides complete visual security (reduces barking at passersby), natural beauty, and 15-30 year lifespan with our wood-on-steel system. Vinyl fencing offers zero maintenance, 35+ year durability, and complete privacy - ideal for busy families. Chain-link fencing is the most affordable, allows airflow and visibility, and works well for dogs who don't react to seeing outside the yard. Most pet owners choose cedar or vinyl for privacy benefits. We'll help you choose based on your dog's temperament, your budget, and aesthetic preferences.

Yes, we specialize in challenging installations. North Shore properties often have slopes, ledges, rocky soil, and uneven terrain. We use stepped fencing for steep slopes (maintaining security at ground level), racked panels for gradual grades, and custom solutions for properties with ledge or large rocks. Unlike DIY installations that leave dangerous gaps on slopes, our professional team ensures continuous ground-level security regardless of terrain. Many competitors won't tackle difficult properties - we see them as opportunities to showcase our 40+ years of problem-solving expertise.

We install commercial-grade, pet-proof gate systems with three security features: (1) Double-latch mechanisms that require two separate actions to open (preventing smart dogs from nudging gates open), (2) Self-closing hinges that automatically pull gates shut even if not fully latched, and (3) Elevated latch placement that's comfortable for adults but out of reach for dogs standing on hind legs. For escape artist dogs, we offer keyed locks or combination systems. All our gate hardware is commercial-grade and comes with our lifetime workmanship warranty.

Dog fencing costs vary based on material, height, length, terrain, and special features. Basic chain-link systems start around $15-20 per linear foot. Cedar privacy fencing typically ranges $35-55 per linear foot. Vinyl privacy systems range $45-70 per linear foot. A typical 150-200 linear foot perimeter for an average backyard runs $5,000-$12,000 depending on material and features. Costs include professional installation, permits, dig barriers, and secure gate systems. We provide transparent, itemized quotes with options to fit different budgets - and every installation includes our lifetime workmanship warranty that competitors charging similar prices don't offer.

In most North Shore towns, fences 6 feet or under in backyards don't require permits. However, regulations vary by municipality: some towns require permits for any fence, corner lots may have sight-line restrictions, and properties near wetlands or conservation areas have additional requirements. Front yard fences are typically limited to 3-4 feet. Don't worry about navigating these requirements - we handle all permit applications and know the specific rules for each town we serve. We ensure your dog fence is compliant before installation begins.

Most residential dog fence installations take 2-4 days, depending on length and site conditions. A typical 150-200 linear foot perimeter is usually completed in 2-3 days. Larger properties or complex terrain may require 4-5 days. We provide a clear timeline in your estimate and communicate throughout the process. Current scheduling is typically 3-4 weeks from quote approval during our busy season (late summer through fall). During slower periods (winter/early spring), we may start within 1-2 weeks. For urgent situations, we can sometimes provide temporary fencing solutions while your permanent fence is scheduled.

Our lifetime workmanship warranty covers any installation defects for as long as you own your home. This includes: post stability issues, gate alignment problems, sagging panels, hardware failures, and structural issues caused by installation methods. This far exceeds the industry standard 1-2 year warranties offered by competitors. Material warranties vary by manufacturer (typically 20-30 years for vinyl, 10-15 years for cedar). The warranty is transferable to new homeowners. We also offer prompt repair service even for fences we didn't originally install, though those repairs aren't covered under warranty.
