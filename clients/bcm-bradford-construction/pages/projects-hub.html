ADU Build - Essex, MA

Accessory Dwelling Unit · New Construction

An accessory dwelling unit built from the ground up on a residential property in Essex, MA. The ADU features a mix of cedar shake and white clapboard siding with a standing-seam-style composite deck and post railing. Inside, the open kitchen pairs white shaker cabinetry with marble backsplash and a natural wood range hood over quartz countertops. Custom tile work runs through the entryway, walk-in shower with bench seating, and a bathroom anchored by a freestanding soaking tub beneath an arched window.

Custom Home Build - Wakefield, MA

Full Rebuild · Addition · Custom Interior

A complete custom home build in Wakefield, MA that took the existing structure from demolition through a fully finished, move-in-ready home. The scope included a new foundation, second-floor addition, full roof system with engineered trusses, and ZIP sheathing throughout. The completed exterior features blue clapboard siding with a rear deck addition. Inside, hardwood floors run throughout, anchored by a custom staircase with oak railing and pendant lighting. The kitchen showcases a hexagon-tile backsplash, while the primary bath includes a skylight shower. Built-in storage, a shiplap mudroom, and a dedicated shelving system round out the finished spaces.

From the Job Site

Rick breaks down how ICF (insulated concrete form) construction works and why we use it for ADU and custom home foundations. Filmed on-site at a current ADU project in North Andover, MA.

ICF foundations provide superior insulation, structural strength, and moisture resistance compared to traditional poured concrete - key advantages for New England construction where freeze-thaw cycles and energy efficiency matter.
