Portsmouth, NH ADU Zoning and Permit Requirements

New Hampshire's ADU laws changed significantly in 2025 with the passage of HB 577. Here's what Portsmouth homeowners need to know before starting an ADU project.

  • ADUs Allowed by Right Under HB 577 (effective July 1, 2025), Portsmouth must allow one attached or detached accessory dwelling unit by right in every zone that permits single-family homes. No conditional use permits or special exceptions are required - just a standard building permit.
  • Size Requirements Municipalities cannot restrict ADU size to less than 750 square feet. Maximum allowable size is 950 square feet unless Portsmouth authorizes a larger unit. This applies to total living space including kitchen, bath, and sleeping areas.
  • Setbacks and Dimensional Standards ADUs follow the same setback, lot size, and dimensional requirements as the primary dwelling. Portsmouth cannot impose additional restrictions on a property simply because it includes an ADU.
  • Existing Structure Conversions Existing structures, including detached garages that don't meet current setback or lot coverage requirements, can be converted to ADUs without zoning relief. This opens up options for many older Portsmouth properties.
  • Owner Occupancy Portsmouth may require that the property owner live in either the primary dwelling or the ADU, but cannot dictate which unit the owner must occupy.
  • Building Permits and Inspections Building permits are required for all ADU construction in Portsmouth. Bradford Construction Management manages the full permitting process on behalf of our clients - from application through final inspection and certificate of occupancy.

Portsmouth may have additional local requirements beyond state minimums. We verify current zoning details with the City of Portsmouth before every project. Have questions about what's allowed on your lot? Reach out for a free consultation.
