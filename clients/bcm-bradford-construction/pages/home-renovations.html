Common Questions

Home Renovation FAQs

Answers to questions we hear most often from homeowners considering renovation projects.

Whole home renovation costs vary significantly based on scope, finishes, and structural changes. Projects typically range from $150,000 to $500,000+ depending on home size and complexity. We provide detailed estimates after assessing your specific goals and property conditions.

It depends on the scope. Kitchen and bathroom renovations often allow you to stay with some inconvenience. Whole home renovations typically require temporary relocation for safety and efficiency. We'll discuss livability options during your consultation and help you plan accordingly.

Timeline varies by project scope. Kitchen renovations typically take 8-12 weeks, bathroom renovations 4-8 weeks, and whole home renovations can take 4-8 months or longer. We provide realistic timelines upfront and keep you informed of progress through daily photo documentation.

Design-build means one team handles both design and construction. This eliminates finger-pointing between architects and contractors, streamlines communication, and typically results in faster completion and fewer surprises. You have a single point of accountability throughout your project.

Most renovation work requires permits, especially for structural changes, electrical, plumbing, and HVAC modifications. We handle all permit applications and coordinate inspections as part of our service, ensuring your project meets local building codes.

Unlike most contractors, we don't disappear after final payment. Through Bradford Home Services (BHS), you have access to ongoing maintenance, seasonal check-ups, and priority service. The team that built your renovation continues to support it for the long term.

Yes, we can execute plans from your architect or designer. However, our design-build approach often provides better coordination and cost control. We're happy to discuss which approach makes the most sense for your specific project.

Older homes often reveal surprises once walls open up. We document everything with daily photos, communicate immediately when issues arise, and present solutions with clear cost implications before proceeding. No surprise bills at the end.

Yes, we have experience with historic North Shore properties. We understand period architecture, work within preservation guidelines when required, and know how to add modern conveniences while respecting your home's character and history.
