Whether it takes the form of a detached backyard cottage, a garage conversion, or an in-law suite addition, an ADU includes its own entrance, kitchen, bathroom, and sleeping area. Ipswich ADU Regulations & Zoning Requirements

Under the Massachusetts Affordable Homes Act (Chapter 40A), ADUs up to 900 square feet are now allowed by right in all single-family residential zoning districts in Ipswich. That means no special permit or zoning board approval is required — your ADU is treated the same as any other residential building project.

Ipswich's Building Department, led by Building Inspector Jim Bone, processes ADU building permits under this by-right framework. Your ADU must comply with existing setback and height requirements, and standard building, electrical, and plumbing permits are required. Owner-occupancy is not required under the state law, and Ipswich is currently developing local regulations around short-term rental use for ADUs.

Bradford Construction Management handles the full permitting process, from initial site evaluation through final inspections, so you don't have to navigate Ipswich's building department or zoning requirements on your own.

Maximum Size

900 sq ft or 50% of primary dwelling, whichever is smaller

Permit Type

By right — no special permit or ZBA hearing required

Owner Occupancy

Not required under state law

Setbacks

Must comply with existing district setback requirements
