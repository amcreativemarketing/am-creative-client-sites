ADU Types We Build in Exeter, NH

Every lot is different, and so is every homeowner's situation. We evaluate your property, talk through your goals, and recommend the ADU type that fits your budget, timeline, and how you plan to use the space.

Detached Accessory Dwelling Units

A standalone structure on your property, completely separate from the main house. Maximum privacy for both homeowner and occupant. Under HB 577, detached ADUs are now allowed by right statewide - no special permits needed in Exeter.

Attached ADUs and In-Law Suites

An addition connected directly to your existing home. Ideal when lot size or budget favor building onto what's already there. We design private entrances, separate living areas, and full kitchens so the space works as a true independent unit.

Garage-to-ADU Conversions

Convert an existing garage or build living space above it. Often the most cost-effective path since the foundation and structure are already in place. NH law now allows conversions of existing structures even if they don't meet current setback requirements.

Basement Conversion ADUs

Turn underused basement space into a fully finished accessory dwelling unit. Basement ADUs maximize your existing footprint without changing the exterior of your home. We handle egress requirements, waterproofing, and mechanical systems.

Backyard Cottages and Studios

A smaller, purpose-built structure for a home office, guest house, or rental unit. Backyard cottages fit well on Exeter lots where a full detached ADU isn't needed but the homeowner wants flexible, independent space.

Exeter, NH ADU Zoning Rules and Permit Requirements

New Hampshire's ADU laws changed significantly in 2025. Here's what Exeter homeowners need to know before starting a project.

Under HB 577 (effective July 1, 2025), Exeter must allow one attached or detached accessory dwelling unit by right in every zone that permits single-family homes. No conditional use permits or special exceptions are required - just a standard building permit. This replaced the previous framework that only guaranteed attached ADUs.
Municipalities cannot restrict ADU size to less than 750 square feet. Maximum allowable size is 950 square feet unless Exeter authorizes a larger unit. This applies to total living space including kitchen, bath, and sleeping areas.
Under the updated law, ADUs follow the same setback, lot size, and dimensional requirements as the primary dwelling. Exeter cannot impose additional restrictions on a property simply because it includes an ADU. This is a significant change from previous rules.
Existing structures, including detached garages that don't meet current setback or lot coverage requirements, can be converted to ADUs without zoning relief. This opens up options for many older Exeter properties with existing garages or outbuildings.
Exeter may require that the property owner live in either the primary dwelling or the ADU, but cannot dictate which unit the owner must occupy. ADUs may be rented as independent dwelling units. Short-term rental rules may be subject to additional local ordinances - confirm with the Town of Exeter before listing on platforms like Airbnb.
Building permits are required for all ADU construction in Exeter. The ADU use must be recorded by deed addendum at the Registry of Deeds, and the lot cannot be converted to a condominium or separate ownership. Bradford Construction Management manages the full permitting process on behalf of our clients. Reach out for a free consultation.

Exeter may have additional local requirements beyond state minimums. We verify current zoning details with the Town of Exeter before every project.
