ADU Types We Build in Exeter, NH

Every lot is different, and so is every homeowner's situation. We evaluate your property, talk through your goals, and recommend the ADU type that fits your budget, timeline, and how you plan to use the space.

Detached Accessory Dwelling Units

A standalone structure on your property, completely separate from the main house. Maximum privacy for both homeowner and occupant. Under HB 577, detached ADUs are now allowed by right statewide - no special permits needed in Exeter.

Attached ADUs and In-Law Suites

An addition connected directly to your existing home. Ideal when lot size or budget favor building onto what's already there. We design private entrances, separate living areas, and full kitchens so the space works as a true independent unit.

Garage-to-ADU Conversions

Convert an existing garage or build living space above it. Often the most cost-effective path since the foundation and structure are already in place. NH law now allows conversions of existing structures even if they don't meet current setback requirements.

Basement Conversion ADUs

Transform an unfinished basement into a fully legal accessory dwelling unit with separate kitchen, bathroom, and living area. Direct egress requirements ensure safety and occupancy compliance. Often the most affordable option.

Backyard Cottages and Studios

Compact, fully-equipped dwelling units designed for your backyard. Single-occupancy studios up to 400 sq ft, or 1-bedroom cottages. Modern finishes and efficient layouts maximize value in a small footprint.

Exeter, NH ADU Zoning Rules and Permit Requirements

New Hampshire's HB 577 (effective July 2025) requires municipalities to allow ADUs by right in residential zones. Here's what that means for Exeter homeowners.

Yes. Under HB 577, Exeter must now allow both attached and detached ADUs by right in all residential zones. This law took effect July 1, 2025, and supersedes any prior local restrictions. Both accessory dwelling units are permitted uses - you don't need a conditional use permit, variance, or special exception, just a standard building permit.

HB 577 allows ADUs up to 800 square feet (and up to 1,000 sq ft if attached to the main house). Attached ADUs can be slightly larger. Basement conversions, garage conversions, and detached cottages all fall under this framework. Exeter's zoning also requires minimum lot size and setback standards, but under the new law, an existing non-conforming structure can be converted.

Exeter has not mandated additional off-street parking specifically for ADUs. However, you should plan for at least one dedicated parking space on your property. The town reviews each application based on the lot's layout and access. Bradford Construction Management coordinates with Exeter's Planning Department to ensure your design meets current parking expectations.

Typical timeframe is 4-8 weeks from submission to permit issuance, depending on whether the Planning Board or Building Department requires additional review. HB 577 streamlines the process since local control options are more limited. We manage all submissions, question responses, and revisions so you don't have to deal with the paperwork.

Yes. Building permits are required for all ADU construction in Exeter. The ADU use must be recorded by deed addendum at the Registry of Deeds, and the lot cannot be converted to a condominium or separate ownership.
