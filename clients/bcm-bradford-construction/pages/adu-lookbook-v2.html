Construction Method

Wood-Framed

From $217,000 (Elm)

Faster construction - Traditional framing methods allow for quicker build times, typically 2-3 months on site.

Lower upfront cost - Standard materials and familiar techniques keep initial investment more accessible.

Easier modifications - Future changes, additions, or repairs are straightforward with conventional framing.

Proven performance - The standard for residential construction with decades of track record in New England climate.

Best for: Budget-conscious projects, faster timelines, or properties where you may want future flexibility.

Construction Method

ICF Construction

From $252,000 (Elm)

Superior energy efficiency - Concrete core with foam insulation delivers R-values 2-3x higher than wood framing. Lower utility bills for life.

Exceptional durability - Concrete walls resist rot, termites, and moisture. Built to last generations, not decades.

Quieter living - Mass of concrete blocks dramatically more sound than wood framing. True privacy from the main home.

Faster on-site build - ICF walls go up in approximately 1 month. Less weather dependency than traditional framing.

Best for: Long-term investment, energy-conscious owners, properties in harsh weather, or aging-in-place plans.

Before You Begin

Is Your Property ADU-Ready?

Every property is different. Here's what we evaluate during your free site assessment to determine feasibility and optimal placement.

1

Setback Requirements

Distance from property lines, main house, and neighbors varies by town. We know the rules for every municipality we serve.

2

Utility Access

Water, sewer (or septic capacity), electrical service, and gas availability. We map the most efficient connection routes.

3

Site Conditions

Slope, drainage, soil type, and existing landscaping all influence placement, foundation type, and overall cost.

4

Zoning & HOA

Local ADU regulations, any HOA restrictions, and permit requirements. Massachusetts law now supports ADUs statewide.

Common Questions

What's not included in the base price?

Site-specific costs like utility connections, excavation, septic (if needed), and landscaping vary by property. We provide detailed estimates after site evaluation.

Do you offer financing?

We work with several lenders who specialize in ADU financing. Options include home equity loans, HELOCs, and construction loans.

How long does permitting take?

Permit timelines vary by town - some approve in weeks, others take months. We handle all submissions and navigate the process for you.

Can I live on-site during construction?

Absolutely. ADU construction happens in your backyard with minimal disruption to your main home.
