Is an ADU Right for You?

ADUs aren't for every situation. Here's how to know if one makes sense for your property and goals.

  • Fully separate entrance, kitchen, bathroom, and often utilities
  • Often faster permitting under new MA and NH ADU laws
  • Adds a flexible income-producing asset to your property

  • ICF build time: ~1 month once permitted

Common Questions

ADU FAQ

How much does an ADU cost in Massachusetts?

Bradford ADUs start at $217,000 for our single-story Elm model (514-600 SF, wood-framed) and $280,000 for our two-story Maple model (726 SF, wood-framed). ICF construction is available for enhanced durability and energy efficiency. Final pricing depends on site conditions, customizations, and finishes. These are turnkey prices including permits, interior electrical, plumbing, and professional installation.

How long does it take to build an ADU?

Once permits are approved and site preparation is complete, ICF construction typically takes about one month. Wood-framed construction may take longer depending on project scope. The full process from initial consultation to move-in ready is usually 4-6 months, with permitting being the most variable factor.

Do I need a permit to build an ADU?

Yes, ADUs require building permits in Massachusetts, New Hampshire, and Maine. Recent state laws have made ADU permitting easier in most communities. Bradford handles all permitting as part of our turnkey service.

Can I rent out my ADU?

In most communities, yes. ADUs can be used for long-term rentals, and some towns allow short-term rentals. Rental regulations vary by municipality. Bradford ADUs are designed with separate entrances and utilities, making them ideal for rental use.

What's the difference between an ADU and a home addition?

An ADU is a separate, self-contained living unit with its own entrance, kitchen, and bathroom. A home addition expands your existing house. ADUs offer more privacy and rental potential, while additions increase your primary home's square footage. We build both.

Can Bradford customize the ADU for my property?

Absolutely. Our models are starting points. Every Bradford ADU is customized to fit your property, needs, and preferences. We offer different configurations, exterior styles, layout modifications, and finish selections.

Does Bradford build ADUs in New Hampshire and Maine?

Yes. Bradford Construction is licensed and insured in Massachusetts, New Hampshire, and Maine. We work throughout the North Shore, Merrimack Valley, Seacoast NH, and Southern Maine regions.

What happens after my ADU is built?

Unlike most contractors, we don't disappear. Through Bradford Home Services (BHS), you have a permanent team for maintenance, repairs, and warranty work for as long as you own your home. This "Bradford For Life" approach means you're never left wondering who to call.
