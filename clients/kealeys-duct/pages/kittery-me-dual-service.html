Is Your Dryer Vent Putting Your Kittery Home at Risk?

A clogged dryer vent isn't just an inconvenience that makes your clothes take longer to dry - it's a genuine fire hazard. Lint is highly flammable, and when it builds up inside your vent line, the heat from your dryer can ignite it. The U.S. Fire Administration reports that dryer fires cause an estimated $35 million in property loss annually, and the vast majority are preventable with regular cleaning. How often you should clean your dryer vent depends on household size and usage, but most homes benefit from annual cleaning at minimum.

In Kittery's older neighborhoods, many homes have long or complex dryer vent runs that are especially prone to lint buildup. Vents that travel through walls, ceilings, or crawl spaces collect lint at every bend and joint. If your dryer takes more than one cycle to fully dry a load, the outside vent flap doesn't open when the dryer runs, or you notice a burning smell during operation, it's time for professional dryer vent cleaning.

Warning Signs Your Dryer Vent Needs Cleaning

Extended Drying Times Clothes taking two or more cycles to dry completely signals restricted airflow from lint buildup.
Hot Dryer or Laundry Room Excessive heat on the dryer's exterior or in the surrounding area indicates exhaust isn't venting properly.
Burning Smell A burning odor during operation is a serious warning that lint may be overheating inside the vent line.
Lint Around the Dryer Visible lint accumulating behind, around, or on top of your dryer means the vent system isn't capturing exhaust properly.
Vent Flap Won't Open The exterior vent hood should open when the dryer is running. If it stays shut, airflow is blocked.
It's Been Over a Year The NFPA recommends annual dryer vent cleaning as a minimum. Larger households may need cleaning more frequently.

How Often Should Kittery Residents Schedule Cleaning?

For dryer vent cleaning, the NFPA recommends at least once per year. Households with larger families doing more laundry loads per week may benefit from cleaning every six to nine months. For air duct cleaning, every three to five years is a good baseline for most homes, though households with pets, smokers, allergy sufferers, or recent renovations may want to clean more frequently.

Maine's climate adds another factor. The long heating season means your HVAC system runs heavily from October through April, pushing more air through your ducts and accelerating the buildup of dust and debris. Scheduling your duct cleaning before heating season begins helps ensure clean air circulation throughout the coldest months.

Can dirty air ducts make my allergies worse?
Yes. Dust, pollen, pet dander, and mold spores accumulate inside ductwork and get recirculated every time your HVAC system runs. For allergy and asthma sufferers, this can significantly worsen symptoms. A professional cleaning removes these irritants from your system, and many customers report noticeable improvement in their breathing and allergy symptoms.
Is air duct cleaning really worth it?
When done properly, yes. The key is hiring a reputable company that performs a thorough cleaning of your entire system, not just a quick pass over visible vents. We provide before-and-after photos on every job so you can see exactly what was in your ducts.
What's the difference between air duct cleaning and dryer vent cleaning?
Air duct cleaning focuses on your HVAC system - the network of ducts that distribute heated and cooled air throughout your home. This impacts your indoor air quality and system efficiency. Dryer vent cleaning addresses the exhaust vent that carries hot, moist air and lint from your dryer to the outside of your home. This is primarily a fire safety service. Both are important, and many homeowners schedule them together for complete home maintenance.
