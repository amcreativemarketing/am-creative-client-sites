Warning Signs Your Dryer Vent Needs Cleaning

Longer Drying Times

If your clothes need two or three cycles to dry completely, lint is likely blocking airflow in your vent system. This wastes energy and puts unnecessary strain on your dryer.

Excessive Heat

Is your dryer or laundry room unusually hot during operation? Trapped heat from a clogged vent creates serious fire risk and can damage your dryer's heating element.

Burning Smell

Lint is highly flammable. If you notice a burning odor when your dryer is running, turn it off immediately and call us—this is an emergency.

When Should You Schedule Duct Cleaning?

The EPA recommends air duct cleaning every 3-5 years for most homes, or sooner if you notice any of these signs:

  • Recent home renovations or remodeling (construction dust in ducts)
  • Pets that shed fur or dander
  • Visible dust buildup around vents and registers
  • Musty or stale odors when your HVAC system runs
  • Family members with allergies, asthma, or respiratory issues
  • You've recently moved into an older or historic home—common in Rye

Dryer Vent Maintenance Tips for Rye Homeowners

Between professional cleanings, there are several things you can do to keep your dryer vent system functioning safely and efficiently:

  • Clean the lint trap after every single load—this alone can significantly reduce fire risk
  • Check your exterior vent hood regularly for lint buildup, bird nests, or debris
  • Watch for warning signs like longer drying times, excessive heat, or burning smells
  • Keep the area around your dryer clear of clothing, boxes, and flammable materials
  • In winter, check for ice buildup around the exterior vent—common in New Hampshire's cold coastal winters
  • Schedule annual professional cleaning to maintain full airflow and prevent fire hazards
