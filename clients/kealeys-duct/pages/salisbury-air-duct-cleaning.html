Common Air Duct Problems in Salisbury Homes

Coastal Humidity and Mold Growth

Living near the Atlantic means higher humidity, and that moisture gets into your ductwork. Over time, it can promote mold and mildew that circulates through your home every time the HVAC kicks on. If anyone in your household deals with allergies or breathing issues, dirty ducts make it worse.

Seasonal Allergens and Pollen

Spring and fall in New England bring heavy pollen cycles. That pollen, along with dust and pet dander, builds up in your ducts and keeps recirculating. A professional cleaning makes a noticeable difference — many of our customers tell us they can feel it within days. If you have pets, this is especially worth reading.

Older Home Duct Systems

A lot of Salisbury homes have HVAC systems with ductwork that hasn't been cleaned in years — sometimes never. Add in renovations, aging insulation, and deteriorating duct materials, and there's a lot that can build up. We've been working on older North Shore systems since 1993, so we know how to clean them thoroughly without causing damage.

Clean ducts mean less dust, fewer allergy symptoms, and easier breathing — especially for family members with asthma or sensitivities. Your HVAC system runs more efficiently too, which means lower utility bills during those harsh New England winters and less wear on the equipment.
