Why Portsmouth, NH Homeowners Need Dryer Vent Cleaning

The #1 Cause of Home Dryer Fires

Lint buildup inside dryer vents is the leading cause of residential dryer fires, with the NFPA reporting roughly 15,000 dryer fires each year across the U.S. Portsmouth's older housing stock, including historic homes in the downtown district and neighborhoods throughout the Seacoast, often have longer or more complex vent runs that trap lint more easily. When airflow gets restricted, heat builds up and the conditions for a fire become very real.

How Clogged Dryer Vents Hurt Energy Efficiency

A restricted dryer vent forces your dryer to work harder and run longer to dry each load. That means higher energy bills, more wear on heating elements and motors, and a dryer that wears out years before it should. Many Portsmouth homeowners notice their clothes take two or more cycles to dry, not realizing a simple vent cleaning would fix the problem and cut their energy costs.

Signs Your Dryer Vent Needs Cleaning

Warning Signs to Watch For

If you notice any of these signs, your dryer vent likely has a lint buildup that needs professional attention:

01

Multiple Dry Cycles

Clothes take more than one cycle to fully dry, even on high heat settings.

02

Overheating Dryer

Dryer feels excessively hot to the touch after running a normal cycle.

03

Burning Smell

A burning or musty odor when your dryer is running, caused by trapped lint or moisture.

04

Excess Humidity

Laundry room feels unusually warm or humid while the dryer is on.

05

Visible Lint Buildup

Lint collecting around the dryer vent opening, door seal, or behind the unit.

06

Blocked Vent Hood

The exterior vent hood flap doesn't open when the dryer is running.
