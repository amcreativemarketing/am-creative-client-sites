Why Newburyport Homeowners Need Professional Dryer Vent Cleaning

Lint Buildup Is a Hidden Problem

Every time your dryer runs, small fibers pass through the lint trap and collect inside the vent line. Over time, this buildup restricts airflow, forces your dryer to work harder, and creates a combustible layer of material inside a confined space. According to the U.S. Fire Administration, failure to clean dryer vents contributes to approximately 2,900 home dryer fires each year.

For homes in Newburyport's historic downtown, the Point neighborhood, and along the Route 1A corridor, many dryer vents run through longer duct paths or have multiple bends - both factors that accelerate lint accumulation and increase the importance of regular cleaning.

Kealey's professional dryer vent cleaning services remove the lint and debris your lint trap misses, restoring safe airflow and helping your dryer perform the way it was designed to.

Signs Your Dryer Vent Needs Cleaning

If you notice any of these warning signs, it may be time to schedule a professional cleaning:

01
Clothes take more than one cycle to dry. This is the most common sign of a restricted vent. Your dryer generates enough heat, but trapped moisture has nowhere to go.
02
The dryer feels unusually hot during operation. Blocked airflow causes heat to build up inside the drum and cabinet. This strains the appliance and increases the risk of overheating.
03
A burning smell comes from the dryer area. Lint is highly flammable. If you smell something burning while your dryer runs, shut it off and schedule a cleaning promptly.
04
Visible lint around the exterior vent cap. Check the outside of your home where the dryer exhausts. Lint accumulation around the vent hood indicates restricted flow inside the duct.
05
It has been more than 12 months since your last cleaning. Most home-safety organizations recommend annual dryer vent cleaning. Households with pets or heavy laundry loads may benefit from every 6 months.
